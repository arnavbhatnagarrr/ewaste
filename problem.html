The E-Waste Crisis

Why It's a Crisis

Electronics contain valuable materials — gold, copper, rare earth metals — but also toxic ones: lead, mercury, cadmium. When improperly handled, these toxins leach into soil and water, causing long-term health damage to communities near dumping sites.

India alone generates millions of tonnes of e-waste annually, yet formal collection infrastructure is nearly nonexistent outside major metros. The informal sector steps in — but without proper tools, safety, or data.

The Data Gap

The biggest obstacle to solving e-waste isn't willpower — it's visibility. We don't know where devices go after disposal. There's no national tracking, no grassroots data, no feedback loop. Policymakers can't make good decisions without data. Recyclers can't optimise without data. Communities can't advocate without data.

This is the gap Pavitra Foundation exists to fill.

Manual Efforts Aren't Enough

Collection drives, awareness campaigns, and community education are essential — but they can't scale without a system underneath them. A volunteer driving across a city to collect 30 devices is heroic. Software that coordinates 300 collectors across a region is transformative.

That's why we're building what comes next.
